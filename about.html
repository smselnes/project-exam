How it all started

Traveling across borders to see new places or countries was totally unknown to me as a youngster. I heard about schoolmates going to mediterranean areas with their families in holidays, staying at fancy hotels where you could have something called all inclusive and eat as much food you wanted, or having as much soda or ice cream your stommach could handle.

Inspired by grandma

Going into my teenages, I built an even stronger relationship to my beloved grandma. We were already very close, but as she lived as close to the school as you could possibly do, it was natural to eat dinner or just drop by after school often.

Since my grandpa died when I was around 6 years old and she eventually becoming a pensionist, she developed new interests. One of this was exploring a bit out of Norway, so she joined arranged travel groups with other people in same situation. From all around the country. This way she created friendly relationships which lasted for years. Or they faded slowly away as she later on got a dementia diagnose. Sadly.

New friendships inspires

Finishing high school, highly demotivated from studies i accidently got an offer I couldnt refuse, being offered a full time job as a cook. This was about 900km away from my hometown, but since I had no specific plans it was a no-brainer.

Being settled down in city of Tønsberg, working long and busy days with good colleagues, my social network expanded quickly. Some of them worked as much as they could through most of the year, saving money to spend on traveling around in the world. This triggered something in me, and after about a year I decided to make an application to a folk high school to study tourism.

I packed my bags and went moving to the school located in a very small town called Lena to meet up with my new student friends.

Studying tourism

Getting started on the year of tourism, I quickly realized this was perfect match for me. Throughout the year we traveled multiple times to different destinations. We practiced on being tour guides, both for our fellow students and also for unknown people, being on a trip. It could be buses, boats, trains and while walking at the destination.

A fantastic educational year it was. I got friends for life during the year, which I speak to and meet regularly still today, over 15 years after. I also established contacts within the travel industry. I actually had an direct offer to go work as a cook for 6 months in Turkey straight after school. I didnt take the offer, but Its cool to think about what turn my life would take if I did. No regrets though.

Its all about traveling now

The following years it was pretty much all about hard work in the restaurant back in Tønsberg. Saving money. Spending money. Traveling with friends. Festivals and concerts here and there every summer. Roskilde-festival in Denmark was a must in July annually.

I moved into an apartment with one of my best friends during this period. We shared some interests, and he adapted my interest of traveling. What we didnt know at the time, our lives would soon take turns into other interests. Going from non-serious relationships with women, we both were soon in serious relationships with our girlfriends. We even declined an offer to go working and living in Canary Islands, where they had jobs, apartment and a car available. Well, I've always liked how coincidences have brought us to where we are today.

Not only for the parties anymore

After meeting my girlfriend we quickly merge the interest of seeing new places. Exploring together and having equal priorities and points of interest on a trip is so valuable.

Now, as my daughter has started on school, I try to share some interest both in geography and learning about other countries. She was given a globe with pink light inside from one of her uncles, and she loves pointing somewhere on it, so we can google the place and see pictures from there. She also enjoy "driving" at the place on Google Maps.
